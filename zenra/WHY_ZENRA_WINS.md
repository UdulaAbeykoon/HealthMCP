# Why Zenra Wins — one page for judges

## The problem is real, and it's everyone's
High performers don't burn out because they lack data — they burn out because *acting* on it is one more job. Every wearable today says "your recovery is low" and then leaves you to renegotiate your own calendar at 6am. Zenra closes that last mile: **it doesn't just tell you, it organizes your life around your energy** so you can show up for the meeting, the workout, and the family dinner.

## What we built (and it's functional)
A 10-screen product where the agentic layer is real, not staged:
- **Live Gemini reasoning** — agents reply in character; the Conductor runs a real "morning huddle" that returns fresh, coordinated proposals as structured JSON.
- **Real actions** — approving a proposal **creates/moves Google Calendar events** and sets **Slack Do-Not-Disturb** through live OAuth.
- **A real voice** — the orb speaks your morning briefing via ElevenLabs, and you can talk back (speech-to-text).

## Three things judges will remember

### 1. The Conductor — you can *see* the agents huddle
Most "multi-agent" demos are a single LLM call wearing seven hats. Zenra makes coordination **visible**: a live Timeline of agent swimlanes with animated coordination links, a Network graph of signals flowing through a central Conductor, and a Calendar heatmap of who touched which hour — under a timestamped, fully explainable reasoning trail. _This is the screen that sells the category._

### 2. Coordination, not commands
One light night of sleep cascades through the whole team:
> **Lyra** flags 78% sleep efficiency → **Sage** sees HRV −12% → **Orchid** pushes the 9am to 10:30 → **Echo** holds Slack to match the new focus block → **Atlas** swaps intervals for Zone 2 → **Fern** moves dinner earlier → **Iris** schedules a gentle check-in.
Seven agents, one decision, narrated and reversible.

### 3. Trust by design
A visible **autonomy spectrum** (Observe → Co-pilot → Trusted → Full autopilot) with per-agent overrides and safety gates: low-risk reversible actions can auto-execute; anything touching a manager 1:1 stays approval-gated. Every nudge shows its "Why?". It's not medical advice and it says so.

## Why it's delightful, not clinical
Warm, witty, human copy from named characters with real voices and colors. An icy-blue, conversation-first interface led by a breathing orb. Instrument Serif headlines. A spoken morning briefing as the opening "wow." It feels less like a dashboard and more like a team that has your back.

## The line that lands
> **"Zenra doesn't just remind you. It quietly organizes your life around your energy."**
